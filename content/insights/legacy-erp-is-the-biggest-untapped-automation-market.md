# Legacy ERP is the Biggest Untapped Automation Market

Let’s start with the why.  Most organisations are chasing “digital transformation” in all the wrong places.  They are buying new platforms.  Moving to the cloud.  Running big programmes with big names.

But they are ignoring the one place where the biggest gains already sit.

Their legacy ERP estate.

## The Reality No One Talks About

Legacy systems are not going away.

- Over 70% of global enterprise workloads still run on legacy platforms (IDC)
- More than 60% of organisations say legacy systems are holding back transformation (Deloitte)
- The global ERP market is worth over $50bn+, with a huge proportion tied up in systems over 10–15 years old

And here’s the uncomfortable truth:  Most of those systems still run core operations.

- Finance.
- Billing.
- Orders.
- Supply chain.

They are not “old systems.”  They are mission-critical systems that never got modernised around the edges.

## Where the Real Opportunity Sits

The mistake most organisations make is they try to replace the ERP.  Which can be eye wateringly expensive and take an age to deliver.  This is all high risk, tightrope walking stuff.

And unfortunately most of the time the project fails to deliver the return promised.  So, what is the smarter move?

Leave the ERP alone and fix everything around it.

Because the real problem is not the ERP.

It is what sits on top of it:

- Manual document processes
- Poor customer communications
- Batch outputs with no flexibility
- No integration into modern digital channels

This is where automation delivers value.

## The Commercial Case (This Is the Bit That Matters)

So, what does the research tell us.

- McKinsey estimates 30–40% of operational tasks can be automated today
- Organisations that apply automation to core processes see 20–30% cost reduction
- Customer experience improvements directly impact revenue, with CX leaders growing revenue 2x faster (Forrester)

Now apply those kinds of numbers to legacy ERP environments.  You are not ripping and replacing with high cost and slow projects, you are unlocking value that is already paid for.  That is faster ROI, far clearer outcomes and far lower risk.

## Why This Market is Still Untapped

There are some very simple reasons why this issue remains, the ownership is broken.  ERP sits in IT. Document ownership and process sits in operations or departments.  The customer experience sits somewhere else.  Which means that no one owns the end-to-end process.  So, nothing changes and everyone hangs their hopes on the big “ERP” that may deliver in a few years.

But as we say here at 6 Leaf Clover:  Automation only matters when it changes the numbers on a P&L.  Right now, most legacy estates are not doing that.

## The Shift That Needs to Happen

One of the themes of all our blog posts is that people need to stop thinking about systems.  Instead, they need to start thinking about a singel document process and flow.  Which in turn means that each individual document, process or flow is a project.  So, in very simple terms it may look like this.

- Order → Document → Customer
- Invoice → Output → Payment
- Policy → Communication → Compliance

If you start to fix the process and document flow from legacy, then that is where value lives.  Which is where automation wins.

## And This Is Where It Gets Interesting…

Because once you start looking at the problem this way, something becomes obvious.  You don’t need to replace the ERP.  You need to control what comes out of it.

- Documents.
- Communications.
- Outputs.

That is the control point.  The leverage that delivers automation.  Also the adoption across the business is also far quicker.  Employees are still fundamentally using the same systems, they don’t have to learn new systems the amount of change for an employee is far less and easier to comprehend.

## Final Thought

Most organisations are digging for gold in the wrong place.  The value is not in the new platform, the big ERP project.  It is in what you already have—and how you use it.

Or as Springsteen put it:

> “You can’t start a fire without a spark.”

Your ERP is the spark.  You just haven’t lit it properly yet.
