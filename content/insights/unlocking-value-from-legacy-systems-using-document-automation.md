# Unlocking Value from Legacy Systems Using Document Automation

Let’s pick this up from Tuesday.  We said the biggest opportunity in enterprise automation is not replacing legacy ERP.  It is unlocking the value around it.  Now let’s get our hands dirty...

## The Why

Every ERP system produces one thing consistently.  Documents.  Lots of them.  Billentis estimates that 560 billion invoices were produced globally in 2025.  That’s just invoices!  An ERP system will also produce many other documents, which are also produced in the billions.

This is where a business shows up to the customer. And in most organisations?  Frankly, it is a mess.

## The Problem (And It’s Bigger Than People Think)

The Transactional and sometimes promotional documents are generated in rigid, outdated formats, if you want changes that requires IT effort and long release cycles.  In many cases the Output is fragmented across print, email and digital.  Which ultimately means that there is little control, governance or tracking.

Meanwhile:

- The Customer Communications Management (CCM) market is worth $2bn+ and growing steadily
- Organisations that modernise communications see significant improvements in customer satisfaction and retention
- Poor communication is a top driver of churn in financial services and utilities

This is not a “nice to have”. This is directly linked to revenue, cost and compliance.

## The Missed Opportunity

Most organisations treat document automation as a tool.  They install it. They configure it for a single document or process.  They move on.  And then… nothing really changes.

Why?

Because they never connect it to business outcomes.  Many organisations struggle to turn Objectif Lune capability into clear business value, for all there transactional and promotional documents.  That is the gap.

## What Good Looks Like

When document automation from ERP and Legacy is done properly, three things change.

1. Control Moves Out of IT.  Business teams can own content, templates and changes. That means speed and a better understanding of the departmental business challenges.

2. Output Becomes Consistent.  One platform across print, digital and hybrid channels.  That means consistency, control and compliance.

3. Processes Become Automated.  Documents are no longer outputs. They become part of a workflow and automated.  This applies not just to the customers but also internally.  Getting rid of all those paper pushing process internally.  That means efficiency.

## The Commercial Impact

This is where it gets real.

- Faster billing cycles → improved cash flow
- Better customer communications → reduced churn
- Automated workflows → lower operational cost
- Centralised control → reduced compliance risk

This is not pretend transformation.  This is measurable impact.

## Why Objectif Lune Fits This Perfectly

Because it sits exactly where the value is. Between:

ERP → Output → Customer

It does not require you to replace the ERP.

It allows you to:

- Modernise outputs
- Automate workflows
- Improve customer experience
- Create new commercial services
- Connect to Modern Services and Systems

And importantly:  It turns a technical capability into something that moves the business.  Which is the whole point.

## The Bigger Play (Most People Miss This)

This is not just about efficiency.  It is about growth.  New services for customers.  New revenue streams for partners.  New propositions for vendors.  You are no longer constrained by the wrought iron that is the ERP and Legacy, you can convert promises and not have to employ or redeploy people and build another manual process because the computer had said no.

This is how you turn automation into a revenue engine, not just a cos(t) play.

## Bringing It Back to Tuesday

Legacy ERP is the opportunity.  Document automation is the unlock.

Simple as that.

## Final Thought

Most organisations are sitting on value they cannot see.  Because they are looking at systems, not outcomes.  Fix the output layer.  Everything changes.

And to bring it home, Springsteen again:

“It’s a town full of losers, and I’m pulling out of here to win.”

You don’t need to rip everything out to win.  You just need to unlock what is already there.
